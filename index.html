Introduction

When approaching this project, I knew exactly which archive to evaluate. The Davie County Register of Deeds was vital to the research conducted for my History Capstone. The Register of Deeds archival database houses scanned deeds, mortgages, leases, and other contracts specific to land/ property in Davie County, North Carolina, separated by categories including Full System (Indexing and Imaging Combined Retrieval), Imaging System Only, and Scanned Index Books. The categories are also divided by date, ranging from 1837 to the present. Using this archive, I cross-referenced it with the U.S. Census Bureau and North Carolina Department of Education records to confirm the identities of African American landowners and to identify the factors that shaped the lives of those who did and did not own land during the Jim Crow era.

Mission Statement

There is a ‘Welcome Message’ that reads like a mission statement:

“All of us here at the Register of Deeds office remain committed to protecting and preserving Davie County’s records in order to secure their integrity for all time. We remain ready and willing to help all citizens and will continue to be knowledgeable in our field, courteous to our patrons, and efficient in our work to bring you remarkable customer service.”

Background

The Davie County Register of Deeds is not an open-source, web-based application. Instead, the database is privately hosted by Logan Systems Inc. in Greensboro, North Carolina, and provides equitable access to official public records. The state government hosts the leading information page, which serves as the entry point to this and other databases. On the leading page, the state provides links to various databases under the jurisdiction of the Register of Deeds, including vital records, marriage licenses, notary public, and others.

To enter the separately hosted database, click the “Search Online Records” option. Upon entering the database, the introductory page offers another welcome message, stating that Davie County has updated the website to improve efficiency for county residents and all site users. There is an important message that, when clicked, is dated 2023. The last option before opening the database is a legal disclaimer stating that the documents on the site are unofficial records and that, if users need official documents, they must visit the Davie County Register of Deeds office in North Carolina. Clicking the blue-highlighted acknowledgment opens the next page, taking the user to the database.

Objective of Investigation

For this project, the fictional client is the Davie County Register of Deeds office. The office has recently been inundated with inquiries and complaints about user experiences using the database. They have reached out for an independent system analysis to identify issues affecting user experience and provide actionable suggestions to improve the system. The independent report will be forwarded to the site host, Logan Systems Inc., for proposed updates. To achieve this goal, Nielsen’s Heuristics are used to identify issues affecting user experience, usability tests are conducted with a panel of five participants, and the patron complaints and inquiries are examined and organized to address specified user engagement difficulties.

Evaluation of the Current Database

The most significant system weaknesses in comparison to Montgomery County, Maryland’s Digital Repository for Land Records include the following Nielsen’s Heuristics.

Nielsen’s Heuristics

1. Visibility of System Status – Communicate to users the current status of the system. Keeping users informed builds trust in the system, fostering repeat use.

3. User Control and Freedom – Supporting the ‘undo’ and ‘redo’ site functions gives users a quick exit in case of mistakes made during use. Providing a clear “exit” to ensure users return to the home screen or previous page fosters a sense of freedom and confidence in the user’s ability to use the system.

4. Consistency and Standards – Follow platform and industry conventions. Users’ experiences with other products shape their expectations when using your system. Therefore, maintaining industry consistency is paramount to decreasing users’ cognitive load.

7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use – Create ‘shortcuts’ for seasoned users that will enhance the user’s ability to tailor frequent actions.

10. Help and Documentation – Help and Documentation should be displayed and easily accessed. Common questions can be answered, links to resources, and contact information provide a way for users to interact with site administrators.

Solutions

Visibility of System Status – On the North Carolina government’s homepage, a sitemap is available to help users navigate the system. Including a sitemap in the database would help users navigate the system, establish transparency, and increase search efficiency.

User Control and Freedom – Although the ‘Back’ function is supported on the Davie County Register of Deeds site, a more visible “Home” button would help users return to the beginning of their search if a mistake or misstep occurs. The ‘Home’ button on the database is almost invisible. Currently, the comparable Maryland State Land Records website, https://landrec.msa.maryland.gov, includes a ‘Home’ button to provide users with control and freedom clearly visible on the top banner.

Consistency and Standards – The banner menu should be visible, especially since there are no CSS options available for users to customize the site for personal use. The Maryland Land Records site includes a highly visible banner menu, with colors to improve visibility. The banner menu on the Davie County Register of Deeds is small, and its options are almost invisible. The banners also include different options, and if Davie County has the ability to offer residents and citizens more options that align with industry standards, like “Contact Us” or “Indices,” it is highly recommended to do so.

Flexibility and Efficiency of Use – The Davie County Homepage includes CSS options for the site’s usability. Users can optimize the site for personal use. The Maryland Land Records site offers users account creation options to promote repeat use and personalization. Providing users with CSS options and account access would increase repeat use and enable them to personalize the site for more efficient use.

Help and Documentation – There is no ‘Help’ option anywhere on the Davie County Register of Deeds database. There must be both a ‘Help’ option and a ‘Contact Us option. This is paramount for transparency, critical for trust, and will help system administrators track and fix user issues. The Maryland Land Records offer users a ‘Help’ option that includes site guidance, ‘User guides,’ and ‘Frequently Asked Questions.’ Offering this option in the Davie County Register of Deeds databases would also positively impact users' perception of the site.

Usability Test Results

Participants were asked to search and retrieve specific documents from the Davie County Register of Deeds website. They were given 20 minutes to locate two documents. The usability test revealed it was very difficult for novice users to navigate the database.

  • Please locate the deed to the property owned by Abner Steel (1883).
  • Locate the canceled debt and deed for Henry Patterson (1908).
  • Search parameters include name and date of deed. Variables such as grantor, grantee, book, and page are not provided; the participant may provide these details once the deed is located.

Usability test observations – The site is difficult to navigate for new users and takes considerable time to become acclimated to. Only two of the participants were able to locate the Henry Patterson record in Book 176, page 20. The other participants were not successful and found the database confusing. Participants sought out guidance and instructions, but could not find them. The 20-minute time frame encouraged efficient rather than aimless searching. Participants attempted to ask questions to aid their search, to no avail. Participants also complained about the lack of transcription options. Updating the system to include transcribing options would increase usage by patrons and researchers.

Patron Inquiries & Complaints

Patron complaints include 40% requesting a ‘Help’ or ‘Contact Us’ option added to the database, while 45% of the inquiries asked for guidance and instructions explaining how to use the database. User experience should be maintained positively to encourage repeat use and promote the database. Updating the database to include these changes would greatly benefit site users and build trust, letting repeat users know their concerns were heard.

Another 15% of complaints and inquiries are specifically about searching the database, and the results their searches return. Although patrons need instructions, they are also seeking simple updates, like identifying pages in search results. Currently, there is no indication that the results that populate are pages in a book.

The search results populating on the left side of the screen should be larger, and the results should be labeled for understanding.

Conclusion

In conclusion, analyzing the website/database has revealed that five of Nielsen’s Heuristics have been identified for user improvement. Improvements for user engagement and repeat use have been provided based on Nielsen’s Heuristics. A usability test was also conducted, and the results align with the Heuristics identified. Only 2 of the 5 participants completed the usability test and successfully located the requested items. To minimize user frustration, recommendations were made to introduce document-transcribing software. Finally, patron complaints and inquiries were examined and quantified, and align with the user Heuristic recommendations. The Davie County Register of Deeds can improve user feedback and engagement by implementing the recommendations in this report.
